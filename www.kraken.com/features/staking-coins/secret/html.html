        FAQ

        Secret staking FAQ:

        1. Can I convert SCRT to USD?

          You can easily convert SCRT to USD by taking the price of Secret multiplied by the number of SCRT tokens you hold. See our Secret converter for details.

        2. What is proof of stake?

          Proof of Stake is a consensus algorithm that uses cryptocurrency tokens and network incentives to keep a blockchain secure. On a Proof of Stake blockchain like Secret, SCRT tokens are staked in order to make network validators eligible to verify transactions and incentivize their honest behavior. The amount of SCRT a validator stakes is directly proportional to the likelihood that they will be chosen to validate the transaction, with some degree of randomness. Those chosen to verify a transaction on a Proof-of-Stake blockchain like Secret are rewarded with SCRT.

        3. Can I stake SCRT using my hardware wallet?

          It is not possible to stake Secret on Kraken using your hardware wallet. If you already have SCRT in a hardware wallet, you can easily transfer it to Krakens secure staking platform. 

        4. How do I run a Secret validator node?

          Running a Secret validator node is a technically complex and costly process which requires a significant amount of resources. Because many feel the investment in running a Secret node is too great, they often choose to stake their SCRT using an easy and secure service like Kraken.

        5. What is the difference between on-chain and off-chain staking?

          While similar in name and design on our client interface, on-chain and off-chain staking are separate features. On-chain staking utilizes assets On-chain in Proof-of-Stake protocols such as Secret. Off-chain staking utilizes assets Off-chain (as further described in our Terms of Service). You can view your eligibility for Off-chain staking here.
